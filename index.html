Hello, I am Greg Medlock

I'm a PhD student in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at the University of Virginia, where I am advised by Dr. Jason Papin.

I am broadly interested in quantitative modeling of biological systems and how interactions at small scales (e.g. molecular interactions between a protein and another compound) lead to the actions we interpret at larger scales (e.g. growth and division of bacterial cells).

I use this blog to share and learn about technical aspects of my field of research and ways to "do science". Blog posts may span a large range of "meta"-ness, from notes about interactions between science and society to useful snippets of code, with a few posts about my hobbies peppered in.
