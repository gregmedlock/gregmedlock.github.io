About me

I grew up in Puyallup, Washington and studied bioengineering at the University of Washington. In 2014 I moved to Charlottesville, Virginia where I entered the University of Virginia Biomedical Engineering PhD program.

I am currently in Dr. Jason Papin's Computational Systems Biology Laboratory, where I study metabolism in microbial communities. One of my primary interests is advancing the tractability of hypothesis testing with gnotobiotic animals (animals colonized with a defined, limited set of microbes), such as the altered Schaedler flora.

I was initially drawn to science through exercise, and I maintain this interest by training and competing in powerlifting. You can find my competition results here.
